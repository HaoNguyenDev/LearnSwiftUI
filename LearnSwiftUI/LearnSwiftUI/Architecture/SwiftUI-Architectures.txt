1. MVVM + Clean Architecture (Most Balanced)
This is the most popular architecture and is considered the "sweet spot" for most medium to large applications.

Structure: Combines MVVM (UI management) with Clean Architecture classes such as Repositories (data processing) and Use Cases (containing business logic).

Why use it: * Takes good advantage of SwiftUI's @StateObject and @Observable mechanisms.

Easy to learn and recruit staff because most iOS developers are familiar with it.

Clear separation between logic and interface, making unit testing easier.

Scaleability: Good for teams of 3-10 people.

2. The Composable Architecture - TCA (Powerful & Easiest to scale for complex apps)
If you are building a super app or an app with extremely complex state, TCA is the "champion".

Structure: Based on the Redux (Unidirectional Data Flow) philosophy. Everything revolves around State, Action, and Reducer.

Why use it: * Consistency: The entire app operates according to a single rule, making it extremely easy to debug.

Modularity: You can break the app down into dozens of independent "Features" and assemble them like Lego pieces.

Absolute testability: TCA is designed so that every line of logic is testable.

Scaleability: Excellent for very large projects with multiple teams working in parallel. However, it has a very steep learning curve.

3. MV (Model-View) - "Vanilla SwiftUI" (Best for small/medium-sized apps)
Since Apple launched the Observation and SwiftData frameworks, this minimalist architecture has become increasingly powerful.

Structure: Eliminates the traditional ViewModel, instead using intelligent Models (Observables) directly within the View.

Why use it: Extremely concise code, maximizing SwiftUI performance without overlapping intermediate layers.
Scale potential: Suitable for start-up projects requiring speed-to-market.
